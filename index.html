I'm a recent graduate in Robotics and Digital Systems Engineering from Tecnológico de Monterrey, Campus Querétaro, where I graduated with honors and an academic talent scholarship. My work sits at the intersection of robotics, computer vision, and machine learning — I like systems that have to actually work in the real world, not just in simulation.

I've built digital twins for human-robot collaboration research at the University of Victoria, led autonomous navigation and driving-stack projects using ROS2, LiDAR, and computer vision, and worked on production data pipelines at GE Aerospace. Alongside the technical work, I served as Vice President and later President of the Citizenship Secretariat within FETEC, my university's student government executive committee.

I care about writing clean, well-documented systems, about rigorous testing in simulation before hardware, and about explaining technical work clearly — whether that's a conference paper, a team briefing, or this website.
