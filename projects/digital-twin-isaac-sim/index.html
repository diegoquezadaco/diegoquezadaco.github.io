Digital Twin for Robotic Cells in NVIDIA Isaac Sim

University of Victoria, ACIS Lab — real-digital twin unification for human-robot collaboration (HRC) research.

Date 2025 — 2026
Duration 4 months
Role Research Intern, Mitacs Globalink
Organization ACIS Lab, University of Victoria
Technologies Isaac Sim, ROS2, Python

Overview

This project developed a unified framework connecting a physical robotic cell with its digital counterpart in NVIDIA Isaac Sim, enabling real-time synchronization for human-robot collaboration (HRC) research. The work was carried out at the ACIS Lab, University of Victoria, as part of a Mitacs Globalink Research Internship, and later presented at CSME 2026 in Vancouver.

Responsibilities

  • Designed and implemented the real-digital twin synchronization pipeline between the physical robotic cell and its Isaac Sim counterpart.
  • Built and validated the ROS2 interfaces bridging simulated and physical robot state.
  • Ran and analyzed experimental trials to quantify synchronization accuracy and latency.
  • Authored the technical paper and built the full presentation delivered at CSME 2026.

Technical Challenges

  • Minimizing end-to-end latency between physical robot state changes and their reflection in simulation.
  • Maintaining joint-space accuracy under real-time constraints across repeated trials.
  • Ensuring the digital twin remained robust to sensor noise and communication delays inherent to real hardware.

Results

  • Achieved a joint-space RMSE of 0.037 rad between the physical robot and its digital twin.
  • Measured an end-to-end latency of approximately 363 ms, averaged across ten trials.
  • Findings presented at CSME 2026 (Vancouver) and are being extended into a journal submission to TCSME.
