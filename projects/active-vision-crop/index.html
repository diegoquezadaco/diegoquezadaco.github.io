Active vision teleoperation for crop inspection

Undergraduate research on active-vision teleoperation for agricultural inspection robots, combining operator input with vision-guided viewpoint control to improve crop coverage and inspection reliability.

The problem

Manual teleoperation of an inspection robot is slow and misses occluded plants; the operator can only look where they think to look, not where the crop actually needs attention.

Approach

Combined operator commands with an active-vision layer that adjusts the camera viewpoint based on what the vision system detects, improving coverage without fully removing the human from the loop.

Results

Improved inspection coverage and reliability over manual-only teleoperation in field trials, as part of ongoing AIR Lab research.
