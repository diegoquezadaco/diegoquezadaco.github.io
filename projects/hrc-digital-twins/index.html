The problem

Human-robot collaboration research needs a way to test control and safety strategies without constant physical risk, while still trusting that simulation results transfer to hardware. Most digital-twin setups drift from the real robot over time, or only sync one direction.

Approach

Built a bidirectional synchronization layer between a physical manipulator and its Isaac Sim twin, streaming joint states and environment updates over ROS2 with sub-400 ms round-trip latency. Validated tracking accuracy across ten trials covering different motion profiles.

  • ROS2 bridge between physical controller and Isaac Sim scene graph
  • Joint-space error tracked continuously against ground truth
  • Latency budget profiled end-to-end, not just simulation step time

Results

Achieved a joint-space RMSE of 0.037 rad and end-to-end latency of roughly 363 ms across all ten trials — accurate and fast enough to support HRC safety-monitoring experiments entirely in simulation before touching hardware. Results presented at CSME 2026 in Vancouver; being extended into a TCSME journal submission.
